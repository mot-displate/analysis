01
Revenue Breakdown — System-Attributed vs Code-Attributed
Influencer revenue splits into two attribution paths: system-tracked orders (where the platform directly identifies the source) and code-attributed orders (where customers use a creator's voucher code at checkout). Code-attributed revenue represents a growing share — from 12% of influencer TOV in 2020 to 39% in 2026 — as creators increasingly drive action through codes rather than tracked links, especially on video-first platforms like TikTok and Instagram Stories.

Why is the code share growing? Influencers increasingly use their unique voucher codes as the primary CTA rather than tracked links — codes work natively across all platforms (TikTok, Instagram Stories, YouTube) regardless of session tracking. As the mix has shifted toward video-first creators, code-driven purchases have become a larger share. Each code is tied to a specific creator, so individual performance attribution is fully preserved — enabling per-creator ROAS ranking and spend optimisation.
02
ROAS & CIR — Above Breakeven, but the Trend Is Downward
The channel remains above breakeven: ROAS 1.60× in 2025 and 1.22× in 2026 YTD. CIR is 62.5% (2025) and 80.4% (2026) — high but the channel generates value in most months. The structural concern is the trajectory: ROAS has declined from 5.7× in 2019 to 1.22× in 2026. At current pace, the channel will genuinely cross below 1× within 1–2 years without intervention.

03
Code vs System Customers: Different Profiles, Both Valuable
Code-attributed customers have a consistently lower AOV than system-attributed customers — averaging €22 less per order across 2025–26. They are buying with a voucher in hand, which makes them more discount-motivated. But they skew more new: codes add +4pp to the new customer share, bringing the full channel to ~61% new customers. These are genuine first-time buyers reached through creator content.

March 2026 — The One Month Below Breakeven
March 2026 is the only month in 16 where ROAS falls below 1× (0.94×). CIR hit 106.8%. Spend was €531k — the highest in Q1 — against only €497k in total revenue. The code-attributed share (€186k, 37.5%) is in line with other months, meaning the problem sits in the system-attributed orders collapsing while spend increased. This points to deal-level or creator-level underperformance, not a channel-wide signal.

06
What the Data Says — Six Conclusions
The influencer channel is viable but declining. The data supports a strategic shift — not an exit.
Core finding
The channel is viable — 15 of 16 months above breakeven
With full attribution (system + code): 15 of 16 months are above breakeven, 2025 ROAS is 1.60×, 2026 YTD is 1.22×. The ROAS decline trend is real and should be managed — but the channel generates value on an annual basis and in nearly every month.
Key concern
CIR is high — above the ≤40% total channel target
CIR is 62.5% (2025) and 80.4% (2026 YTD). The channel is not in "spending more than earning" territory for most months, but it operates at a structurally higher cost than performance marketing. This is expected for an awareness/acquisition channel — but the gap needs to narrow, not widen.
Key concern
ROAS structural decline continues — 1× is 1–2 years away
ROAS trajectory: 2020 5.31× → 2021 2.47× → 2022 2.80× → 2023 1.97× → 2024 1.88× → 2025 1.60× → 2026 1.22×. Downward every year except 2022. At current pace, the channel will genuinely cross below 1× within 1–2 years. The direction calls for strategic action now, while the economics still allow it.
Action needed
March 2026 is an anomaly — needs a root cause at creator level
March is ROAS 0.94× — the sole month below breakeven. Spend was €531k (the highest in Q1) while total revenue was only €497k. The code contribution was proportionally normal. The problem is system-attributed orders collapsing while spend increased. This is a deal execution failure that needs to be diagnosed at the per-creator level.
Strategic asset
Per-creator code data enables precise spend optimisation
Code-attributed TOV has grown from 11.9% (2020) to 39.2% (2026). Each code maps to a specific creator, so per-creator ROAS is fully calculable. This data enables ranking creators by true attributed revenue and concentrating spend on the ones that actually convert — moving from blended averages to precise, creator-level investment decisions.
Strategic asset
Influencer customers are genuinely new — the LTV case is real
Code buyers have lower AOV (€121 vs €143) but are more likely to be new customers (+4pp). The full channel brings ~61% new customers at an effective CAC of €125–178. At 1.22× ROAS and €135 AOV, the economics support a first-purchase LTV case — provided retention rates are tracked and validated.
